Why Create a CV Early?

A CV is much more than a document for applying to jobs. It is a powerful tool for self-assessment and professional growth.

Building your CV early allows you to honestly evaluate your current professional profile:

  • What technical skills do I actually have?
  • What projects have I completed?
  • What software can I confidently use?
  • What leadership experience have I gained?
  • What practical engineering experience do I have?
  • What achievements am I proud of?

Very often, students discover that many sections of their CV are almost empty. That realization is valuable—it helps you identify where you need to improve long before graduation.

A Living Document

Treat your CV as a living document. Update it regularly whenever you gain:

  • New technical skills
  • Certifications
  • Leadership roles
  • Engineering projects
  • Industrial training
  • Volunteering experience
  • Competitions
  • Research
  • Workshops and conferences

Over time, you will clearly see your own professional growth.

Don't Wait Until Opportunities Arrive

Many students begin writing a CV only after a job, internship, scholarship, or graduate programme is announced. By then:

  • They are rushing
  • They forget achievements
  • They produce lower-quality CVs
  • They have little time to tailor the document

Instead, prepare early. When opportunities arise, you should simply:

  • Refine the CV
  • Tailor it to the position
  • Highlight the most relevant experiences
  • Submit a polished application confidently
